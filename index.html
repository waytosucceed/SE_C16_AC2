Rounding Off Integers

Rule for Rounding Off:

To round off integers, identify the digit at the place value you wish to round to and look at the digit immediately to its right.

  • If the next digit is 5 or higher, round up by increasing the digit at your rounding place by one and change all lower place values to zero.
  • If the next digit is less than 5, round down by keeping the digit at your rounding place the same and change all lower place values to zero.

Examples:

  1. Round 438 to the nearest ten:
    • The ones digit is 8, so round up.
    • Rounded result: 440
  2. Round 282 to the nearest hundred:
    • The tens digit is 8 (greater than 5), so round up.
    • Rounded result: 300

Rounding Up Integers (Ceiling)

Rule for Rounding Up: When rounding up integers, increase the number to the next highest number at the rounding place value, regardless of the digits that follow.

  • Change all lesser place values to zero and increase the specified place value by one, if necessary.

Examples:

  1. Round up 123 to the nearest ten:
    • Directly increase the ten's place from 2 to 3.
    • Rounded result: 130
  2. Round up 199 to the nearest hundred:
    • Increase the hundred's place from 1 to 2.
    • Rounded result: 200

Rounding Down Integers (Floor)

Rule for Rounding Down: When rounding down integers, decrease the number to the next lowest number at the rounding place value, regardless of the digits that follow.

  • Change all lesser place values to zero.

Examples:

  1. Round down 127 to the nearest ten:
    • Decrease the number by ignoring the units place.
    • Rounded result: 120
  2. Round down 275 to the nearest hundred:
    • Keep the hundred's place as 2.
    • Rounded result: 200
Rounding Off Decimals

Rule for Rounding Off:

To round off decimals, look at the digit in the decimal place you are rounding to, then check the digit immediately following it.

  • If this next digit is 5 or higher, round up by increasing the digit in the rounding place by one.
  • If the next digit is less than 5, round down by leaving the digit in the rounding place unchanged.

Examples:

  1. Round 2.653 to the nearest tenth:
    • The hundredth digit is 5, so round up.
    • Rounded result: 2.7
  2. Round 3.141 to the nearest hundredth:
    • The thousandth digit is 1, so round down.
    • Rounded result: 3.14

Rounding Up Decimals (Ceiling)

Rule for Rounding Up: When rounding up decimals, regardless of the next digit, increase the last retained decimal place by one.

  • This method ensures the number is never less than the original decimal.

Examples(continued):

  1. Round up 0.234 to the nearest tenth:
    • Regardless of the hundredth place being 3, round up to the next tenth.
    • Rounded result: 0.3
  2. Round up 1.115 to the nearest hundredth:
    • Although the thousandth place is 5, rounding up at the hundredth.
    • Rounded result: 1.12

Rounding Down Decimals (Floor)

Rule for Rounding Down: When rounding down decimals, drop all digits beyond the decimal place you are rounding to, regardless of what these digits are.

Examples:

  1. Round down 5.789 to the nearest tenth:
    • Ignore the digits beyond the tenth place.
    • Rounded result: 5.7
  2. Round down 2.673 to the nearest hundredth:
    • Ignore the thousandth place.
    • Rounded result: 2.67

Rule for Rounding Off Decimals to Nearest Integer:

To determine whether to round up or down when rounding a decimal to the nearest integer, focus on the tenths place, which is the first digit to the right of the decimal point.

  • If the tenths digit is 5 or higher, round up the integer part (the part before the decimal) by one.
  • If the tenths digit is less than 5, keep the integer part the same.

Examples:

  1. Rounding 14.7 to the nearest integer:
    • Look at the tenths place, which is 7.
    • Since 7 is greater than 5, round up.
    • Rounded result: 15
  2. Rounding 8.3 to the nearest integer:
    • The tenths place is 3.
    • Since 3 is less than 5, keep the integer part the same.
    • Rounded result: 8
